Methodology

The Brookhart Labs Side-Quest Generator is a research-oriented ideation system designed to surface small, buildable opportunities from observable developer and product discourse. It is not a market forecasting tool, sentiment engine, or investment framework.

Objective

The objective is to translate explicit or implicit expressions of unmet demand into concise, actionable “side-quests” suitable for individuals or small teams. Each idea is intentionally constrained to avoid speculative or over-scoped work.

Data Sources

Signals are derived exclusively from publicly accessible sources where builders articulate needs organically:

  • GitHub issue threads and feature requests
  • Public developer posts on X
  • Open technical discussions and articles

Signal Extraction

Raw text is parsed for language indicating friction, absence, or desire for tooling. Phrases such as “missing,” “wish there was,” “cannot easily,” or “feature request” are treated as candidate signals.

Filtering & Classification

Signals are filtered for specificity, feasibility, and independence from proprietary data. Each signal is classified into one or more categories:

  • Infrastructure — tooling, frameworks, SDKs
  • Product — UX and workflow improvements
  • Research — exploratory or analytical needs

Synthesis

Each idea is structured into a standardized card containing: title, origin, problem, quest, audience, difficulty, and attribution. Difficulty reflects estimated effort to prototype.

Limitations

The generator does not assess commercial viability, adoption probability, or long-term sustainability. Ideas are starting points, not recommendations.

Ethics & Privacy

All inputs are public. No private, gated, or user-restricted content is used. Source attribution is preserved via outbound links.
